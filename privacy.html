Privacy Policy

Zenith · Effective July 6, 2026 · Last updated July 6, 2026

The short version. Zenith is a UV-index and tanning app with no accounts, no ads, no analytics, and no tracking. We operate no servers and do not collect, store, or sell your personal information. The app uses your device's location only to look up the UV forecast for where you are, and your preferences stay on your device.

Who this policy covers

This policy applies to the Zenith iOS app (“Zenith,” “the app,” “we,” “us”). It explains what the app accesses, why, and the very limited cases where data leaves your device.

Information the app accesses

Location (approximate)

When you use the app, it requests your location so it can fetch the UV-index forecast for your area. The app requests reduced (kilometer-level) accuracy — it does not need or use your precise position. Your coordinates are:

  • Sent to Open-Meteo (our weather-data provider) solely to return the forecast for that location.
  • Passed to Apple’s geocoding service to convert the coordinate into a nearby place name (e.g., “Honolulu”) shown in the app.

Your location is used only to service these requests. We do not store your location, associate it with any identifier, or use it to track you. There is no account to link it to.

App settings

Your preferences — UV alert threshold, skin type, and whether notifications are on — are stored locally on your device. They never leave your device and are removed when you delete the app.

Notifications

If you enable alerts, Zenith schedules local notifications on your device based on the forecast. These are generated entirely on-device. We use no push servers, and no device token or notification data is transmitted to us or anyone else.

Information the app does NOT collect

Zenith does not collect, request, or transmit:

  • Names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any account/registration details (there is no sign-up)
  • Contacts, photos, calendars, health records, or files
  • Advertising identifiers (IDFA) — the app shows no ads
  • Analytics, usage/behavioral data, or crash reports (there is no analytics or crash-reporting SDK)
  • Any data used for tracking across apps or websites. Zenith does not track you, so it presents no App Tracking Transparency prompt.

How the app uses information

The only processing is functional: your approximate location is used to retrieve and display the UV forecast, compute tanning windows and a burn-time estimate, and schedule the alerts you ask for. That’s it.

Third-party services

Zenith relies on two third parties strictly to provide its core function:

  • Open-Meteo — provides the UV-index forecast. The app sends latitude/longitude to Open-Meteo’s API to receive the forecast. No account, API key, or personal identifier is sent. See open-meteo.com/en/terms.
  • Apple — standard iOS location and geocoding services provided by your device’s operating system. Apple’s handling is governed by the Apple Privacy Policy.

We have no other third-party SDKs, trackers, or data partners.

Data retention

We retain nothing, because we operate no servers and receive no personal data. Settings and any cached forecast live only on your device and are deleted when you uninstall the app. Any processing performed by Open-Meteo or Apple is subject to their own practices, linked above.

Data sharing and sale

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with anyone. The only data transmission is the approximate location sent to Open-Meteo and Apple to fulfill your request, as described above.

Your choices and controls

  • Location: Allow, limit, or deny access anytime in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Zenith. Without location, the app can’t fetch a local forecast.
  • Notifications: Turn alerts off inside Zenith, or in iOS Settings → Notifications → Zenith.
  • Delete everything: Removing the app deletes all of its on-device data.

Children’s privacy

Zenith is rated 4+, is not directed at children, and does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your region). Because the app collects no personal data and requires no account, there is nothing for a child to submit to us.

Your rights (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA and similar)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict the processing of your personal data, and to not be discriminated against for exercising them. Because Zenith does not collect or store your personal data on any server we control, we hold nothing to access, export, or delete — the data the app uses stays on your device and under your control. We do not sell or “share” personal information as those terms are defined under U.S. state privacy laws. For questions about your rights, contact us below.

Security

Requests to the weather service are made over encrypted connections (HTTPS). Because no personal data is stored on servers we control (we have none), there is no server-side store to breach. On-device data is protected by iOS’s standard app sandboxing and device security.

Changes to this policy
